# La Casa de Bits

## Desc
El Pablo — a tortoiseshell cat and the crew's finest hacker — always said the best heists aren't pulled in banks. They're pulled in binaries.
When The Professor's plan to rob the Royal Mint of Spain went sideways, El Pablo managed to intercept three binaries from the security vault before the police closed in. Each one holds a shard of the Mint's keys. He smuggled them out tucked under his fur, left a note, and vanished into the streets of Madrid.
The note read:

"Three vaults. Three locks. Three lies. The first vault hides its treasure behind a machine that thinks in its own language. The second vault will smile and hand you something — don't trust it. The third vault has the memory of a goldfish and needs the first two vaults to be opened first. Bella Ciao."
